Dancing with the Ex-Military Mountain Man

Dancing with the Ex-Military Mountain Man

I storm in with a paintball gun aiming for justice. He dances me into his arms and refuses to let go.

I drag my brother to Ramsey’s Roadhouse to fix the humiliating prank he pulled on an innocent woman. I do not expect to catch the eye—and the full, heated attention—of Tigg Ramsey, the owner. Six-foot-five. Tattooed. Ex-Marine. And now determined to keep me!

He doesn’t flinch at my limp or scars. Doesn’t pity me either, and I’m helpless against his rough brand of charm. Then he vows I’m the strongest woman he’s ever met and proves his strength by lifting me into his arms and dancing me across the floor as if my body weighs nothing. For the first time since the accident that stole my ballet career, I feel weightless again. Free.

“You were made to fly, angel,” he murmurs, spinning us through the crowd. “And I’m never letting you hit the ground again.”

One dance floor. One starlit night on his rooftop.

And suddenly I’m ready to let the mountain man claim me any way he wants.
