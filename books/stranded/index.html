Stranded with the Ex-Military Mountain Man

I show up at his door drenched, desperate, and not wearing any panties.

Some vacation this is. All I wanted was to finally write my romance novel. Only the deluge of the century strands me in pouring rain with no transportation, no food, and severe chocolate cravings. The kind of hunger that drives me to bang on a stranger’s door.  

The snarly, grumpy mountain man who answers is all brooding glares and reluctant hospitality. His scowl doesn’t hide the pain in his eyes—or the way they flare once he’s fed me and I tell him what I’m not wearing beneath his borrowed shirt.

“I’m too broken for someone like you,” he whispers in the darkness after nightmares drag him from sleep and I crawl into his bed determined to heal his heart, to banish the memories that haunt his nights. 

And if it takes turning up the heat so he’ll stop pushing me away, want to keep me forever? I’m all for it.
