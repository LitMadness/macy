Craving the Ex-Military Mountain Man

Craving the Ex-Military Mountain Man

Every Tuesday, the off-limits mountain man comes into my bakery and pretends to read the newspaper. Ten months of stolen glances and cherry pie, and we’re both done pretending.

As the middle, least-likely-to-make-waves Baker sister running our Gimme Some Sugar bakery, I’ve learned to ignore the whispers that have shadowed my family for years. But there’s one man I can’t ignore—Royce Tanner, the rugged ex-military mountain man who appears every week like clockwork.

I’m wholly aware of his presence as he quietly sips his coffee and watches my every move. The careful way he treats Herman, his anxious rescue dog, makes my heart melt, and I can’t help but wonder if he’d be just as gentle with me.

But neither of us acts on the simmering attraction, Royce being as careful of my reputation as he is with his skittish dog.

Then comes the night of the K-9 Rescue charity auction, and the protective older man I can’t stop secretly craving is finished being careful.

“I’ve waited long enough,” he growls, carrying me away in front of the entire town. “You’re mine now, Civility Baker. Mine to care for and love.”

Small-town gossip and the age gap be damned. I want him and the future his eyes promise, no matter the cost.
