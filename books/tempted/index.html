Tempted by the Ex-Military Mountain Man

I deliver cookies to the reclusive mountain man’s cabin—and by morning, I’m the one being devoured.

My life is a string of impulsive decisions. Bad ones. The worst? Taking off with the wrong guy after high school. He wrapped his hands around my throat and nearly stole my voice for good.

Years later, I’m back in Wild Rose, running Gimme Some Sugar with my sisters and pretending my raspy whisper doesn’t bother me. I make deliveries. I avoid trouble. I definitely avoid men.

Until I’m sent to the remote mountain home of Holden Bentley—former military, current metalsmith, and he absolutely did not order these cookies.

But when I finally get him to open the door (cussing helps), he’s six-and-a-half feet of shirtless, scowling, unfairly gorgeous trouble.

“I’ll protect your secrets,” the emotionally scarred, spiritual ex-Marine promises during the long, dark night we stay up talking after I get stranded. “Teach you to trust again. If you’ll let me.”

And that’s a problem, because I’m beyond tempted to do just that.
